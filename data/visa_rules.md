# Japan Visa Rules

## Overview

Japan offers visa-free entry to citizens of many countries for short-term tourism. However, visa requirements vary depending on nationality.

Travelers should always verify the latest requirements through the official Japanese embassy or consulate in their country before booking flights.

## Tourist Visa Duration

- Typical stay period: 15 to 90 days
- Purpose: Tourism, visiting friends or family, short business visits
- Employment is strictly prohibited under a tourist visa

## Required Documents

Common requirements include:

- Valid passport with at least 6 months validity
- Completed visa application form
- Recent passport-sized photograph
- Confirmed round-trip flight reservation
- Hotel reservations or invitation letter
- Bank statements proving sufficient funds
- Detailed travel itinerary
- Proof of employment or business ownership

## Financial Requirements

Although no fixed amount is officially published, travelers should demonstrate sufficient funds for:

- Accommodation
- Transportation
- Daily expenses
- Emergency situations

A recommended guideline is:

- USD 100–150 per day per traveler

## Entry Requirements

Visitors may be asked to provide:

- Return or onward ticket
- Accommodation details
- Proof of financial capability
- Contact information during the stay

## Customs Regulations

Travelers must declare:

- Cash exceeding JPY 1,000,000
- Restricted medications
- Valuable items requiring declaration

Strictly prohibited items include:

- Illegal drugs
- Weapons
- Counterfeit products

## Important Notes

- Overstaying a visa can result in fines, detention, or future entry bans.
- Travel insurance covering medical emergencies is strongly recommended.
- Immigration officers have final authority regarding entry approval.
