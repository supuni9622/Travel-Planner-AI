# Transportation Guide for Japan

## Overview

Japan has one of the world's most efficient public transportation systems.

Main transport options include:

- Shinkansen (bullet trains)
- Local trains
- Subways
- Buses
- Taxis
- Domestic flights

## IC Cards

Popular prepaid transportation cards:

- Suica
- PASMO
- ICOCA

IC cards can be used for:

- Trains
- Subways
- Buses
- Convenience stores
- Vending machines

## Shinkansen

The bullet train network connects major cities including:

- Tokyo
- Kyoto
- Osaka
- Hiroshima

### Popular Routes

- Tokyo → Kyoto: approximately 2 hours 15 minutes
- Tokyo → Osaka: approximately 2 hours 30 minutes

## Japan Rail Pass

The Japan Rail Pass may provide savings for travelers making multiple long-distance trips.

Consider purchasing a pass if you plan to visit several cities within a short period.

## Tokyo Metro

Tokyo's primary rail operators:

- Tokyo Metro
- Toei Subway
- JR East

Useful apps:

- Google Maps
- Japan Travel by NAVITIME

## Taxis

- Safe and reliable
- Expensive compared to public transportation
- Automatic doors are common

## Airport Transportation

### Narita Airport (NRT)

Options:

- Narita Express
- Keisei Skyliner
- Airport limousine bus

### Haneda Airport (HND)

Options:

- Tokyo Monorail
- Keikyu Line
- Airport limousine bus

## Transportation Tips

- Avoid rush hours if possible.
- Last trains usually depart around midnight.
- Stand on the correct side of escalators depending on the region.
- Keep mobile phones on silent mode.
